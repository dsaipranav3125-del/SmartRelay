SmartRelay AI

Tagline: Predict failures before they happen.

One-line description: SmartRelay AI watches inverter relay behavior in real time and estimates when a relay is likely to fail, so teams can act before downtime.

Theme: Predictive maintenance for inverter relays using AI
Live demo: https://smartrelayapp.web.app/

2. Problem Statement

In many inverter installations, relay failures are discovered only after service drops. Teams can see raw readings, but they usually do not get a clear prediction of remaining relay life.

The result is reactive maintenance: urgent technician dispatch, replacement under pressure, and avoidable operational loss.

Pain points

  • No practical “failure in X days” signal for service planning.
  • Field teams are forced to respond after disruption begins.
  • Hard to prioritize risk across multiple deployed inverters.

Real-world impact

  • Downtime and SLA breach risk.
  • Emergency replacement + technician cost.
  • Business loss example: prevented failure saves ₹50,000 downtime cost.

3. Proposed Solution

SmartRelay AI combines live telemetry with a lightweight prediction model to estimate relay Remaining Life (%) and Failure Risk (%). It then converts this into simple actions: warning, critical, and service recommendation.

The design is split by purpose: edge logic for immediate local alerts, and cloud logic for prediction quality, trends, and fleet visibility.

Prediction objective

  • Inputs: temperature, load %, switching cycles
  • Outputs: Remaining Life (%), Failure Risk (%), Confidence Score, and “failure predicted in X days”

5. System Architecture

Implemented architecture flow:

Inverter → MODBUS → IoT Device → MQTT → Cloud → AI Model → Dashboard

Inverter + MODBUS

Registers expose relay temperature/load/current/voltage and switching behavior.

IoT Device + MQTT

Packages telemetry and streams it reliably to cloud ingestion.

Cloud + AI Model

Computes failure risk and remaining-life estimates continuously.

Dashboard

Turns predictions into alerts and technician actions for operations teams.

6. Model & Technical Approach

The current system uses a linear-regression-style risk scorer. We chose this deliberately: it is easy to explain, fast to run, and stable for real-time updates.

Inputs are temperature, load %, and switching cycles. Outputs are Remaining Life (%), Failure Risk (%), and Confidence Score. The same pipeline can be upgraded later to Random Forest or Gradient Boosting without redesigning the dashboard.

Inputs / outputs

  • Inputs: Temperature, Load %, Switching cycles
  • Outputs: Remaining Life (%), Failure Risk (%), Confidence Score
  • Explainability: contribution breakdown for “Why did it fail?”

Real-world feasibility note

Tested with simulated inverter data aligned to MODBUS protocol ranges.
